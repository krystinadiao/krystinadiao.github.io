This application is still in the process of being created, and the development side is largely under wraps. I will only be documenting the ongoing design process of Climb.gg.

Format: This article will be broken down by weeks. From there, I will break it down into notes, progress, and feedback.
context
League of Legends is an online, multiplayer game with a huge e-sports scene. Competitive teams and players are always looking for new ways to improve gameplay. Climb.gg, a group of 5, aims to build a platform to help them do just that.
my role
I serve as the sole designer on the team, bridging the gap between software engineers, product, and design. I am in charge of determining the user experience and designing the user interface, branding elements, style guides, etc.

1 Designer, 4 Developers
research
  • Understand target audience throughout fast-paced iterations of designs and product decisions
  • Conduct user research such as concept mapping, usability testing, etc.
value prop
  • Provide a platform for users to analyze matches, view and create events, and keep notes
  • In-depth statistics and graphs to strengthen understanding of team and player performance
  • Auto-generated events based on algorithms and data mining
process
  • Collaborate with an engineering-based team to make product decisions and design decisions
  • Meet weekly to discuss updates in work and adaptations in ideas
  • Present to analysts of target teams to discuss new ideas, iterations, and epics
takeaways
  • The product is still under development - I hope to continue updating this design log, within the NDA
01

week 01

My first step, after being brought on to the team, was understanding the product better. Understanding the functionalities, various use cases, target audience, etc. The first two pages I would be in charge of designing are the game details page and the main landing page.
notes
  • Primary use case: you want to explain the game in further detail
  • Elements: play button, map, timeline, events
  • Auto-generated events (e.g. team fights, major objectives, etc.)
  • User-defined events: option to create new event with description & title, based on time
  • On the landing page, there should be some kind of list or icons of games you want to view, click to go into more detail to the game details page
Existing, simple layout of the functionalities
progress
Sketches of initial ideas
  • Unsure about the capabilities, if there will be a VOD (gameplay video) in addition to the map, or just the map
  • Contemplation between in-line “create new event” or modal “create new event”
feedback
  • For now, we are focusing on putting out an initial product, and some of the more complex features are on hold — this includes the VOD, filtering by anything other than point in time (when creating a new event)
  • Change “title” to “name” and “type”, as the type of event will essentially tag or group the event.
  • The current time (on the timeline, as the user is watching) will be auto-filled when creating a new event, and it can be editable
  • Modal vs. Inline: we decided on inline, as modal restricts the user from easily going back and forth between the timeline and the event creation
  • Inclusion of champion stats, including HP, mana, XP at the given point in time (items will be put on hold until later)
02

week 02

notes
  • Removing items that are being held off until later
  • Making adjustments to the “create a new event” input options
  • Adding in champion stats
progress
Sketching out ideas of layout view, timeline player design, champion stats, etc.
While designing the champion stats, I wanted to keep in mind the in-game design, and stay aligned with it since users are already familiar with that HUD interface
Sketch, med-fid mockups showing expanded main menu, expanded game details menu, collapsed menus
My thoughts were to incorporate a double collapsable menu system, one being the main navigation menu of the entire site, one being the menu to access and edit game details. Only one can be expanded at a time. When both are collapsed, the map and game info are expanded, and there is the option to “quick-add” an event based on the live time on the timeline.
feedback
  • Instead of hamburger menu, identifying the main menu with a “C” or a logo, and arrow for the game details menu
  • Need to make it clearer that only one is expandable at a time, rather than both being hamburger menus
  • Having “Create a new event” at the bottom may cause potential problems if the number of events in the events bank is over flowing, and user have to constantly scroll to the bottom to access create new event. Instead, maybe have the create new event option collapsed until called upon, and then it would appear above the existing events
  • Next steps: In addition to taking on the above feedback, the team would like for me to start exploring creating a style guide (logos, iconography, typography, color schemes, etc.) for the look and feel of the site
03

week 03

notes
  • Begin developing a style guide (including logo, typography, color scheme, etc.) for the overall web app to follow
  • Flesh out the design a bit more
  • Making small changes to the interface
progress
Starting to develop the style guide proved as a bit of a challenge. Though I’ve done logo designs and designed overall styles and feels for sites previously, I had never done anything so comprehensive. I was overwhelmed with the options and possibilities. I began by doing a bit of reading on creating a style guide and viewing some example guides:
  • All About Creating Design Style Guides
  • Intro to Style Guides
  • Your Closet is Your Personal Style Guide
  • Medium Brand Development
  • Designing a Style Guide
Given the limited timeframe, and the sprint coming to a quick end, I wanted to ensure I had all the elements created and thought out before taking the time to create a beautiful style guide / booklet. First, I started with the color palette. I had a small idea of what I wanted to incorporate. For a few different reasons, I wanted to go with a dark base: the analytic nature, the need to continuously watch the gameplay panel, and the trademark dark colors of League. Dark backgrounds allow longer viewing periods with less strain on the eyes. I also wanted to go with a deep blue & gold scheme to link recognizability with League of Legends. I identified the 5 main colors I was interested in working off of.

The A Process for Building Out Color Palettes article helped me out a lot along the way. I used her practice of identifying which colors I wanted in my final design.
  • Red: conveys power, success; gives a feeling of using this analytics tool will bring you success in game, leverage the analytics for better results
  • Deep blue/gold: relation to League





I then began fleshing out some of my designs to better place out some of the colors and see what actually fits.

Exploring “Tabbed” Sidebar vs. Two Sidebar
Fleshing out the interface design
feedback
  • The team loved the color choices! I ultimately decided to put the red/orange aside, as it proved to be too bright for the rest of the designs. Because I would also need to include red and blue for the team colors on the gameplay details, as well as green, blue, and purple for the HP, MP, XP bars, I decided to keep Climb’s actual colors more neutral and limited. Too much color would look messy and distract from the gameplay.
  • There is still work to be done on placing the serif & sans serif fonts. I will continue to play around with that placement and integration.
  • Start thinking of solutions to searching for games, keeping in mind the use case would likely be “find me another game with a team fight (event) like this one)
  • The double sidebar may be difficult to scale from an engineering perspective. We explored a new option: carousel at the bottom of the page. This would allow more intuitive and natural navigation of events, as it would already be in chronological order, matching the timeline. It would also allow more space horizontally for us to grow functionalities (for example, eventually showing items for each champion, or more stats for each player).
