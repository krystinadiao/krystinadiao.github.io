01

context

This project was completed during my summer 2016 internship at Bazaarvoice, over the course of about 2 months. Each intern at Bazaarvoice has a focal summer project, this knowledge base analysis being mine.

Bazaarvoice has several different products, most supported with a strong knowledge base and knowledge material; however, one free product, Connections, did not have a knowledge base implemented. With the product knowledge team feeling a lack of personnel and support, it was valuable to have someone come in and prove, quantitatively, what exactly a knowledge base has on the effect of not only the product, but also the various teams in the company.
Goal: Assess the reduced load on support with the introduction of a client self-service knowledge base.
02

my role

My role was primarily researcher and analyst of this project. With this being my primary internship project, it was an excellent opportunity to practice my project management, research, and data analytics / visualization skills. Throughout the entire process, I practice data collection through Salesforce, developing key performance indicators and metric values, conducting user research of various stakeholders, drawing conclusions and actionable insights, and creating the data visualizations.

I also had to opportunity to work with mentors from teams all across the board, from Support (who are primarily affected by the implementation), Product Management (to guide me through D3.js as well as the Connections product itself), Product Knowledge (who created the knowledge material and have historical data with other product knowledge bases as well), etc.
03

research

user personas
After speaking with the primary stakeholders - engineers using the DB, managers overseeing the DB - a couple personas and use cases were identified.

The primary user personas and use cases would be software engineers who are familiar with the EMOdb functions. Thus, the interface does not need much explanatory elements, but should ensure a simple and intuitive flow of information.

04

value prop

This project is valuable in a few different ways:
  • Allows us to show the quantitative value of knowledge bases - how much time and money it saves Bazaarvoice, how much time it saves each team and department, and the type of effect it has on these various teams
  • Brings to light valuable metrics with precise numbers and visualizations
  • Allows the CTO to assess the true value of the Product Knowledge team, and prove the necessity to increase spending for this team
05

process

Data collection
  • The data collection process consisted of beginning with Salesforce support data - discovering trends and discovering which KPIs needed to be measured
  • The next step was speaking with people with more knowledge of Bazaarvoice and the process than me, for example, Support teams who deal with the actual clients and Product Knowledge members who write the knowledge base
Data visualization
  • Given the loose requirements of the project, I decided to take this beyond just a simple Excel Pivot Table project
  • I wanted to take on a challenge that would allow me to present the data in a more effective way, while also allowing me to learn a new, valuable skill
  • I decided to tackle D3.js - a JavaScript library that allows amazing data visualizations
Presentation
  • I had the opportunity to present this project alongside Bazaarvoice's other summer interns at Intern Demo Day - it was extremely positively received, particularly intriguing the CTO, who decided to present my data at various C-level meetings
  • You can view my project featured on Bazaarvoice's blog post for Intern Demo Day
